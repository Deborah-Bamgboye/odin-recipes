German Hamburgers

Description

Forget everything you know about the American burger bun—meet the Frikadelle (also known as Buletten or Fleischpflanzerl). These are traditional German pan-fried meat patties that are a staple in German households and beer gardens alike.

Unlike a standard burger, Frikadellen use soaked bread and eggs in the mixture, resulting in a texture that is incredibly fluffy on the inside while maintaining a delicious crispy crust. They are traditionally served with spicy mustard, warm potato salad, or a crusty roll.

Ingredients

  • The Meat: 1 lb (500g) Ground meat (a mix of 50/50 beef and pork is traditional for flavor and fat content).
  • The Binder: 1 stale bread roll (or 2 slices of white bread) soaked in water or milk, plus 1 large egg.
  • Flavor Base: 1 medium onion (very finely diced), 1 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped).
  • Seasoning: 1 tsp salt, ½ tsp black pepper, 1 tsp paprika (sweet or spicy), 1 tsp mustard (German or Dijon), pinch of marjoram (optional).
  • Frying: 2 tbsp oil or clarified butter (Buttterschmalz).

Steps

  1. Prepare the Bread: Soak the stale bread in water or milk for about 10 minutes until soft. Squeeze out all excess liquid tightly with your hands.
  3. Sauté Onions (Optional but Recommended): For a milder flavor, sauté the diced onions in a little butter until translucent and let them cool. If you prefer a sharper bite, you can use them raw.
  4. Mix: In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, squeezed bread, egg, onions, parsley, mustard, and spices. Use your hands to knead the mixture until well combined and slightly sticky.
  5. Shape: Wet your hands slightly (to prevent sticking) and form the mixture into 4–6 large, thick patties.
  6. Fry: Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Fry the patties for about 4–5 minutes per side until they are dark golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Serve: Eat warm with mustard and potatoes, or cold on a sandwich the next day.
